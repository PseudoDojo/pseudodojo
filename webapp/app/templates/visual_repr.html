Arctan of the logarithmic derivatives

From the oncvpsp documentation: The plots show ϕ(E)=arctan(R∗dψ(r)/dr|R) for some R greater than the core radius, where ψ is the solution of the non-local radial equation regular at the origin (i.e., the outward-integrated solution). For a well-designed pseudopotential, ϕ(E) will closely track that of the all-electron potential over a wide range of energies from well-below to well-above the valence semi-core states of interest. The steps of π indicate localized pseudo wave functions. Spurious steps of π indicate "ghost" states, which are localized states than on investigation turn out to have more nodes than appropriate for their energies. For GW pseudos, no significant deviation should be present up to 8 Hartree.
